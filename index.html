Who Am I?

I am an architect, modeling the universe

What Am I?

In My Work

I am currently a Ph.D. student at University of Michigan in Ann Arbor. I am working on the possibilities of finding new Higgs physics at the Large Hadron Collider at CERN. I am always finding ways to use machine learning tools in my research. With the large amount of data collected by the ATLAS particle detector, there is always a need for better techniques to comb through large sets of data and find interesting trends.



Visit My Research Group

In My Free Time

I spend most of my time visiting different places around the world, playing sports with my friends, and exploring new skills on my own. I have learned many instruments including piano, percussion, viola, cello, guitar, and the ukulele. My favorite musical genres are classical and electronic. I also like to experiment in the kitchen. My dishes range from imitations of traditional recipes to unique creations. I enjoy making food for others and encourage feedback, but most of the time I cook because I love to eat. I like to swim, snowboard, play ultimate frisbee, and rock climb as well as try out less common indoor and outdoor activities.

Where Am I?


I am currently pursuing my PhD degree at the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor. My work will take me between Ann Arbor, Geneva, and other ATLAS collaboration institutes.

When away from work, I will be spending most of my holidays in Arizona and California visiting friends and family. Despite the brutal summer heat, I greatly enjoy the pleasant and scenic hikes that the southwest has to offer in the cooler seasons.
