What Am I?

My name is Andy Chen and I am currently a Ph.D. student in physics at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. My interests lie in theoretical physics, specifically M-theory, and I plan to write my doctoral thesis on topics in this field. I received my B.S. in physics at Georgia Tech in 2018.

I spend most of my time with my friends and family, playing music, cooking, and doing outdoor sports. I have played piano and percussion since elementary school and have fiddled with viola, cello, guitar, and the ukulele. My favorite musical genres are classical and electronic. I also like to experiment in the kitchen. My dishes range from imitations of traditional recipes to unique creations. I enjoy making food for others and encourage feedback, but most of the time I cook because I love to eat. The more popular sports, such as basketball, baseball, football, and soccer, don't usually interest me. However, I love to swim, snowboard, and play ultimate frisbee as well as try out less common outdoor activities.

Where To Find Me?


I started my PhD career in September 2018 and will be living in the Ann Arbor area for the foreseeable future. I am usually on the UMich Ann Arbor campus or a 15 minute drive away at home.

Raised in the southwest, I will be spending most of my holidays in Arizona with friends and family. Despite the brutal summer heat, the desert offers many pleasant and scenic hikes during the winter and spring seasons.
